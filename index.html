    HUMAN-CENTERED ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND WORKFORCE DISPLACEMENT

    AI has the potential to increase economic growth and encourage innovation, but it also sparks worries about unemployment caused by automation. A study conducted by PwC in 2018 revealed that nearly 40% of jobs in developed nations could be highly susceptible to automation by the year 2030. In order to tackle this issue, it is necessary to carefully cultivate AI technologies, emphasizing the importance of AI centered around humans.

    • Redesigning Jobs and Workflows for AI Integration: Embracing AI that prioritizes human well-being involves treating data science as a field that values privacy and ethics, and working alongside users to improve processes instead of completely automating them. This frequently involves changing job duties and processes, utilizing AI to enhance human efficiency rather than eliminate jobs. Employees must receive training in order to effectively collaborate with AI assistants. Sectors such as manufacturing need to find a balance between AI automation and human supervision to make sure that functions involving decision-making and analytical thinking are still done by humans.
    • Focusing on Human Skills for Career Resilience: In terms of career development, human-centered AI highlights the significance of distinctively human abilities such as creativity, critical thinking, communication, and management. Jobs that depend on these capabilities will be better able to withstand AI interference. Education and training that target these skills will be crucial. The increasing importance of developing and managing AI systems will lead to the growth of roles, yet "AI manager" positions should encompass more than just technical skills. They need to support human interests by finding ways for AI to improve human capabilities instead of displacing employees. Strategic implementation is crucial.

    AI technology is evolving with a focus on keeping people at the center. It involves looking after people as workers, customers, and members of society in a world where machines are getting smarter. Economic advantages and productivity improvements should not be achieved at the cost of human dignity and employment opportunities. By applying principles centered around humans, we can utilize AI's capabilities to elevate humanity.
